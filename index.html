RIDE

Story

...While there wasn't many examples to go off of and the documentation was buried in Class components (my project used Functional Components) using whatever small amount of information I could find scattered everywhere, I destructured, processed and rebuilt it to suit the projects needs. With that information I could create a bookings system unique to each user and each ride.

YELP ME OBI-WAN

Browse Star wars related businesses

While using Yelp you can search for restaurants and various businesses. Setting up a Star Wars theme proved difficult because of the lack of descriptive businesses or restaurants. Maximizing quality was important because of the small quantity/obscurity of Star Wars related content.


List your business

Following the motto of quality, I used a small amount of data fields. In the case of creating/listing a new business, I wanted to try something other than a modal since throughout the site modals are used in abundance. I wanted to explore React and see what other options there were. I settled on using a Menu, similar to when you click the profile icon. In hindsight, I should have probably just used a modal as I ran into several problems with event listeners and styling.


Reviews

By far my favorite feature of the site. Using a package called swiper and integrating it into modals, I created a modal worthy of (I hope) many a Star Wars fan awe. The modal contains a businesses info and related reviews and only allows a user to edit or delete their own review.
